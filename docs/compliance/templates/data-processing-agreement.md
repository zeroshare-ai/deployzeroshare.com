## 1. Definitions

**"Personal Data"** means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person.

**"Processing"** means any operation performed on Personal Data, including collection, storage, use, disclosure, or deletion.

**"Data Subject"** means the individual to whom Personal Data relates.

**"Sub-processor"** means any third party engaged by Processor to process Personal Data.

**"Applicable Data Protection Law"** means all laws relating to data protection applicable to the processing, including GDPR, CCPA, and other relevant regulations.

**"Services"** means the services provided by Processor to Controller as described in the underlying service agreement.

## 3. Processor Obligations

### 3.1 Processing Instructions
Processor shall:
- Process Personal Data only on documented instructions from Controller
- Inform Controller if an instruction infringes Applicable Data Protection Law
- Not Process Personal Data for any purpose other than the Services

### 3.2 Confidentiality
Processor shall:
- Ensure persons authorized to Process Personal Data are bound by confidentiality obligations
- Limit access to Personal Data to those who need access for the Services

### 3.3 Security
Processor shall implement appropriate technical and organizational measures, including:

- [ ] Encryption of Personal Data at rest and in transit
- [ ] Access controls and authentication mechanisms
- [ ] Regular security testing and assessment
- [ ] Incident detection and response capabilities
- [ ] Data backup and recovery procedures
- [ ] Employee security training

### 3.4 Sub-processors
Processor shall:
- Not engage Sub-processors without prior written authorization from Controller
- Ensure Sub-processors are bound by equivalent data protection obligations
- Remain liable for Sub-processor compliance
- Maintain a list of approved Sub-processors

### 3.5 Data Subject Rights
Processor shall assist Controller in responding to Data Subject requests, including:
- Access requests
- Rectification requests
- Erasure requests
- Portability requests
- Objection requests

Processor shall forward any Data Subject requests received directly to Controller within [5] business days.

### 3.6 Security Incidents
Processor shall:
- Notify Controller of any Personal Data breach without undue delay (within 72 hours)
- Provide information necessary for Controller to comply with breach notification obligations
- Cooperate with Controller in investigating and remediating breaches

Notification shall include:
- Description of the breach
- Categories and approximate numbers of Data Subjects affected
- Likely consequences
- Measures taken or proposed to address the breach

### 3.7 Data Protection Impact Assessments
Processor shall assist Controller with Data Protection Impact Assessments where required, providing:
- Information about Processing operations
- Technical and organizational measures
- Assessment of risks

### 3.8 Audits
Processor shall:
- Make available information necessary to demonstrate compliance
- Allow for and contribute to audits and inspections by Controller or authorized auditor
- Provide audit reports or certifications (e.g., SOC 2, ISO 27001) upon request

---

## 4. Controller Obligations

Controller shall:
- Ensure Processing instructions are lawful
- Ensure appropriate legal basis for Processing
- Provide Data Subjects with required privacy notices
- Respond to Data Subject requests
- Notify Processor of any changes affecting Processing

---

## 5. International Transfers

### 5.1 Transfer Restrictions
Personal Data shall not be transferred outside [EEA/UK/other jurisdiction] unless:
- Adequate safeguards are in place (Standard Contractual Clauses, adequacy decisions)
- Controller has provided prior written authorization
- Transfer complies with Applicable Data Protection Law

### 5.2 Standard Contractual Clauses
Where required, the parties agree to execute the EU Standard Contractual Clauses (Module 2: Controller to Processor) as incorporated herein by reference.

---

## 6. Data Retention and Deletion

### 6.1 Retention
Processor shall not retain Personal Data longer than necessary for the Services or as required by law.

### 6.2 Return or Deletion
Upon termination of the Services or upon Controller request:
- Processor shall delete or return all Personal Data
- Processor shall certify deletion in writing
- Deletion shall occur within [30] days
- Processor may retain copies only as required by law

---

## 7. Liability

### 7.1 Allocation
Each party shall be liable for damages caused by its breach of this DPA or Applicable Data Protection Law.

### 7.2 Limitations
Liability shall be subject to the limitations set forth in the underlying service agreement, except where prohibited by law.

---

## 8. Term and Termination

### 8.1 Term
This DPA shall remain in effect for the duration of the underlying service agreement.

### 8.2 Survival
Obligations relating to confidentiality, data deletion, and cooperation shall survive termination.

---

## 9. General Provisions

### 9.1 Amendments
This DPA may only be amended in writing signed by both parties.

### 9.2 Conflicts
In case of conflict between this DPA and the underlying service agreement, this DPA shall prevail regarding data protection matters.

### 9.3 Governing Law
This DPA shall be governed by [JURISDICTION] law.

### 9.4 Entire Agreement
This DPA, together with the underlying service agreement, constitutes the entire agreement regarding Processing of Personal Data.
