## 1. Purpose

This Information Security Policy establishes the framework for protecting [COMPANY NAME]'s information assets and ensuring the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of data entrusted to us by our customers, partners, and employees.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All employees, contractors, and third parties with access to company systems
- All information assets owned, leased, or managed by [COMPANY NAME]
- All systems, networks, and applications used to process company data
- All locations where company business is conducted

## 3. Policy Statement

[COMPANY NAME] is committed to:
- Protecting information assets from unauthorized access, disclosure, modification, or destruction
- Ensuring compliance with applicable laws, regulations, and contractual obligations
- Maintaining customer trust through robust security practices
- Continuously improving our security posture

## 4. Roles and Responsibilities

### 4.1 Executive Management
- Approve security policies and provide resources
- Champion security culture organization-wide
- Review security metrics and incidents

### 4.2 Security Officer / Security Team
- Develop and maintain security policies
- Conduct risk assessments
- Monitor security controls
- Manage security incidents
- Provide security awareness training

### 4.3 IT/Engineering
- Implement technical security controls
- Maintain secure configurations
- Perform vulnerability management
- Support incident response

### 4.4 All Employees
- Follow security policies and procedures
- Report security incidents and concerns
- Complete required security training
- Protect credentials and access

### 4.5 Third Parties
- Comply with security requirements
- Report security incidents
- Maintain confidentiality

## 5. Security Principles

### 5.1 Risk-Based Approach
Security controls shall be implemented based on risk assessment, considering:
- Likelihood of threats
- Impact of potential incidents
- Cost-effectiveness of controls

### 5.2 Defense in Depth
Multiple layers of security controls shall be implemented to provide redundant protection.

### 5.3 Least Privilege
Access to systems and data shall be granted based on the minimum necessary for job functions.

### 5.4 Separation of Duties
Critical functions shall be divided among multiple individuals to prevent fraud and errors.

## 6. Security Controls

### 6.1 Access Control
- Unique user identification for all users
- Strong authentication (passwords, MFA)
- Regular access reviews
- Prompt deprovisioning upon termination

### 6.2 Data Protection
- Classification of data by sensitivity
- Encryption of sensitive data at rest and in transit
- Secure data disposal
- Data backup and recovery

### 6.3 Network Security
- Firewall protection
- Network segmentation
- Intrusion detection/prevention
- Secure remote access

### 6.4 System Security
- Secure configurations
- Patch management
- Malware protection
- Logging and monitoring

### 6.5 Application Security
- Secure development practices
- Code review
- Vulnerability testing
- Change management

### 6.6 Physical Security
- Facility access controls
- Equipment security
- Environmental controls

## 7. Security Awareness

All personnel shall receive:
- Security awareness training upon hire
- Annual security awareness refresher
- Role-specific security training as needed
- Updates on emerging threats

## 8. Incident Management

### 8.1 Incident Response
- Incidents shall be reported immediately
- Response team shall be activated per procedure
- Incidents shall be contained, investigated, and remediated
- Lessons learned shall be incorporated

### 8.2 Breach Notification
- Data breaches shall be reported per legal/contractual requirements
- Affected parties shall be notified as required
- Regulatory authorities shall be notified as required

## 9. Business Continuity

- Critical systems and data shall be identified
- Backup procedures shall be implemented
- Recovery procedures shall be documented
- Plans shall be tested regularly

## 10. Compliance

### 10.1 Legal and Regulatory
- Applicable laws and regulations shall be identified
- Compliance shall be monitored
- Changes shall be tracked and addressed

### 10.2 Contractual
- Security obligations in contracts shall be identified
- Compliance shall be maintained
- Evidence shall be provided as required

## 11. Third-Party Management

- Vendors shall be assessed for security
- Contracts shall include security requirements
- Vendor security shall be monitored
- Access shall be reviewed and terminated appropriately

## 12. Policy Governance

### 12.1 Policy Review
- This policy shall be reviewed annually
- Updates shall be made as needed
- Changes shall be approved by executive management

### 12.2 Exceptions
- Exceptions require documented business justification
- Exceptions require compensating controls
- Exceptions require management approval
- Exceptions shall be time-limited and reviewed

### 12.3 Violations
- Policy violations shall be reported
- Violations shall be investigated
- Disciplinary action may result from violations
- Repeated violations may result in termination

## 13. Related Policies

- Access Control Policy
- Data Classification Policy
- Acceptable Use Policy
- Incident Response Policy
- Business Continuity Policy
- Vendor Management Policy

## 14. Definitions

- **Information Asset**: Any data, system, or resource of value
- **Confidentiality**: Protection from unauthorized disclosure
- **Integrity**: Protection from unauthorized modification
- **Availability**: Ensuring authorized access when needed
- **Risk**: Potential for loss or harm
